Steve Jobs

Entrepreneur | American Businessman | Founder

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs:

  • 1955 - Steve Jobs was born to Abdulfattah Jandali and Joanne Schieble. He was adopted as a young infant by Paul and Clara Jobs
  • 1960 - In 1960, the Jobs family moved to Mountain View in Santa Clara County, California. Today, this is commonly called Silicon Valley.
  • 1973 - Jobs enrolled at Reed College, but dropped out after a semester. He moved to a hippie commune after dropping out. Jobs later takes a job working for Atari.
  • 1976 - In 1976, Wozniak and Jobs show the first board for the Apple I at the Homebrew Computer Club. On April 1, Jobs, Wozniak and Ron Wayne incorporate as Apple Computer.
  • 1980 - Apple Computer entered the stock market in 1980, making Jobs, already successful, remarkably wealthy.
  • 1986 - Jobs purchased the computer animation division of George Lucas' ILM and incorporates it as Pixar.
  • 2001 - The now successful Apple released the first iPod in 2001. Jobs had no idea how much it would change the company or technology.
  • 2007 - In January 2007, Jobs introduced the item most closely associated with Apple today: the iPhone. The iPhone 3G followed in 2008.
  • 2007 - In January 2007, Jobs introduced the item most closely associated with Apple today: the iPhone. The iPhone 3G followed in 2008.
  • 2010 - In 2010, Jobs announced the release of the first tablet, the iPad, and an improved iPhone, the iPhone 4.
  • 2011 - On August 24, 2011, Jobs resigned from Apple, handing the reigns to Tim Cook. His health had worsened.
  • 2007 - Steve Jobs died at home on October 5, 2011, surrounded by his family.
